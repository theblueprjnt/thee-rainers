01Cue Reading

In sparring — do you see punches arriving, or feel them landing?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

Your eyes are on the gloves, not the body. You react to contact instead of anticipating movement.

Fix

Peripheral tracking shadow. Read the shoulder and hip before the hand moves. Partner initiates with a body tell — you respond to the tell, not the call.

02Kinetic Chain

Do your punches come from your legs and hip — or your shoulder and arm?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

Punches land fast but opponents don't respect them. You gas early from throwing. 76% of punch power starts below the waist — and yours isn't getting there.

Fix

Wall-load drill. Slow-motion cross: push through the rear foot, rotate the hip first, then shoulder. Press fist into wall at endpoint and feel where the force originates.

03Reactive Stiffness

Does your footwork feel springy by round 4 — or flat and heavy?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

Your entries telegraph because the bounce disappears. You get hit more in later rounds — not because you're slower, but because tendon elasticity has depleted.

Fix

Bilateral ankle hops 3×20. Target: ground contact under 200ms. Progress to lateral hops, figure-eight, reactive drop-step. Tendon conditioning — not cardio.

04Range Fluency

Can you generate offence at all three ranges — or do you have one home?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

Opponents locate your home range in round one and take it. Your offence disappears when you're uncomfortable. One home range is a known ceiling.

Fix

Three-zone constraint sparring. 30 seconds forced at each range, generating offence — not surviving. The zone that produces panic is the primary target.

05Defensive Responsibility

When you throw combinations — does your guard hold, or does your chin get exposed?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

You get countered on the way out. You only notice the exposure after the punch lands. Offence and defence are two separate decisions instead of one movement.

Fix

Every combination ends with a defensive movement before reset — slip, roll, or exit angle. No combination ends stationary. Drill until the constraint becomes automatic.

06Transfer to Live

Is your sparring the same standard as your pad work — or does pressure strip the technique?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

Clean technique in drilling is the starting point — not the destination. Your nervous system reverts to older patterns under threat. Transfer has to be built separately.

Fix

Graduated constraint sparring. One element only — you can only slip, no other defence. The constraint forces the technique into live conditions. Progress bi-weekly.

07Identity & Presence

When hurt and behind — do you fight your system, or contract and wait for the round to end?

1 — Breaks10 — Automatic

If low

The self-concept you carry becomes a performance ceiling. Technical systems deployed by a contracted identity produce contracted results.

Protocol

Write 10 attributes of the fighter you are becoming — architectural, not aspirational. Read before every session. For one round, perform as that fighter regardless of outcome.
